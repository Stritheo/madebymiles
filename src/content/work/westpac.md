## Context

Westpac's general insurance operation had two sub-scale provider arrangements inherited from the Westpac and St George brands following the bank merger. Neither had distribution priority, marketing budget, or technology investment. The insurance division generated $2bn in premiums and contributed significantly to earnings, but the partner-manufactured portfolios lacked infrastructure to grow.

Three distinct roles over nearly six years.

## What I did

**Role 1: Product Manager, Sourced (Jul 2009 to Jan 2011).** Assessed the two incumbents' performance, capability, and commercial models. Negotiated improved terms with both providers. Secured performance-linked marketing investment. Worked directly with retail bank executives to commit to volume targets. Built a technology roadmap embedding insurance in bank-branded experiences. Grew the portfolio from $115m ($60m motor, $55m CCI) to $200m.

**Role 2: Senior Manager, Consumer Credit and Sourced (Feb 2011 to May 2013).** Managed a 42-person team. ASIC scrutinised whether CCI products delivered genuine customer value. Rather than defending margins, increased product benefits and built a proactive process to contact customers entitled to claims. ASIC agreed the products were defensible. Led Lean Six Sigma claims process reengineering. Outsourced non-customer-facing operations offshore. Managed line 2 governance across onshore and offshore. NPS from +25 to +44. Staff engagement from 66% to 92% over 20 months.

**Role 3: Senior Manager, General Insurance (Jun 2013 to Feb 2015).** Seconded to GM's office. Originated the strategic recommendation to consolidate to a single alliance partner. Ran a six-month competitive process: six domestic and international insurers shortlisted to two preferred bidders. Wrote the recommendation for the CEO. Developed the distribution strategy and operating model. Allianz selected. Scope extended to replatforming manufactured home insurance.

## Results

- Portfolio grown from $115m to $200m
- NPS from +25 to +44
- Staff engagement from 66% to 92% over 20 months
- Allianz selected as strategic alliance partner
- Distribution strategy and operating model delivered
- ASIC confirmed products were defensible
