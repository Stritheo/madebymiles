## Context

Hollard acquired the CBA general insurance portfolio, creating a 15-year strategic alliance to serve one million customers. The acquisition doubled the size of Hollard's business. My role was to engage the Board and executive on the joint strategy, assess the risk profile of the combined portfolio, and lead critical regulatory and reinsurance workstreams for the entire group.

## What I did

**Portfolio assessment.** What emerged was that CBA Insurance was a better governed and run portfolio than expected. It required some minor underwriting changes to 1% of the portfolio to fit within target risk appetite.

**Board and executive engagement.** Engaged the Board and executive on the joint strategy, risk profile, and integration priorities. Built enduring relationships with directors that have continued beyond the role.

**Operational scope.** Claims and operations were centralised. I managed distribution, product, pricing, and data.

**Technology recommendation.** Recommended a technology architecture and roadmap that would accelerate the integration between Hollard and CBA. The internal alternative was chosen by the CEO and was still being implemented 3.5 years later.

**Regulatory closure.** The ASIC Pricing Promise Review was a single piece of work spanning both CBA and Hollard. Signed the attestation on time at Hollard, based on a control environment that prevented breaches. No regulatory action resulted.

**Cyclone reinsurance.** Led entry into the Australian Government's Cyclone Reinsurance Pool for the entire combined CBA and Hollard group, drawing on a mature regulatory change and reinsurance capability that was further progressed than Hollard's existing book.

## Results

- Combined portfolio assessed and confirmed as well governed with targeted underwriting changes to 1% of policies
- ASIC Pricing Promise Review signed on time (single review spanning both CBA and Hollard), no regulatory action
- Cyclone Reinsurance Pool entry led for the entire combined CBA and Hollard group
- Enduring board relationships maintained
