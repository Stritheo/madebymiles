## Context

Hollard acquired the CBA general insurance portfolio, creating a 15-year strategic alliance to serve one million customers. This was one of Australia's most complex recent insurance transactions. The portfolio required a complete operational transition while maintaining service continuity and meeting target returns and risk appetite.

## What I did

**Regulatory closure.** Signed the ASIC Pricing Promise Review attestation on time, based on a control environment that prevented breaches. No regulatory action resulted. This is the second ASIC attestation across two separate insurers.

**Cyclone reinsurance.** Entered the Australian Government's Cyclone Reinsurance Pool.

**Remediation proposal.** Proposed a comprehensive plan covering pricing discipline, product design, technology simplification, and controls.

**Board relationships.** Built enduring relationships with directors that have continued beyond the role.

The tenure was nine months. Executive and board alignment on the pace and depth of transformation was not achieved. The decision to depart reflected clarity about the conditions under which I create the most value.

## Results

- ASIC Pricing Promise Review signed on time, no regulatory action
- Cyclone Reinsurance Pool entered
- Enduring board relationships maintained beyond departure
