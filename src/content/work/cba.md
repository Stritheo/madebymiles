## Context

CBA's general insurer had fundamental pricing, product, and control failures. Home insurance was unprofitable. Motor had never generated a profit. The pricing model contained inappropriate rating factors. There was no effective monitoring, oversight, or control framework. Combined operating ratio: 101.5%.

Full P&L and balance sheet accountability including budgeting, forecasting, capital management, and reinsurance. 700 staff across underwriting, pricing, product, claims, operations, HR, finance, and technology. One million customers.

## What I did

**Phase 1: Pricing (three-year programme from 2017).** Rebuilt pricing at individual address, vehicle, and consumer level. Identified and removed inappropriate rating factors. Refunded significant customer remediations proactively. Developed a novel rating factor: 10% average premium reduction, 1% loss ratio improvement, up to 35% on home and 25% on motor for best-scored customers. No adverse rating on lower economic groups. CANSTAR Outstanding Value five consecutive years. Signed the ASIC Pricing Promise Review attestation on time.

**Phase 2: Digital (from 2016).** Built channels embedded in the bank's app, website, group CRM, and home buying process. 30% of lending data reused. Quote-to-sale conversion 20% to 50% in home buying channel within three months. Digital sales from 20% to 70%. Headcount held flat.

**Phase 3: Claims and operations.** Redesigned from customer outcome backward. RPA for billing, admin, data entry, correspondence. Qantas workforce planning model for catastrophe surge. Tiered supplier model measuring outcomes, cost, timeframes, and rework rates. Attritional and catastrophe claims settled faster and at lower cost than industry average.

**Royal Commission.** Two witness statements prepared. Took full accountability as the accountable executive. Called off 36 hours before testimony. Zero enforceable undertakings, zero fines, zero capital consequences. Delegated accountability for running the business to my customer operations leader during preparation, briefing the entity board directors on her strengths and where she would need support.

**Board engagement.** Presented quarterly to the APRA-regulated entity board and monthly to its Risk and Audit Committees. Joint papers with the CRO on risk culture and with the Chief Actuary on FCR. Ran a strategic review on claims operations for the Board, earning trust to retain in-house control through transparent due diligence.

**Leadership team.** Designed a 10-person executive team across the full functional span. External hires from other insurers and industries, internal promotions, cross-functional moves, and exits. Weekly leadership team meetings, monthly business reviews, quarterly strategy sessions, set-cadence 1:1s. The team stayed in place after divestment.

**Reinsurance.** Placed $1.1bn growing to $1.9bn annually. Presented in Singapore, London, and Bermuda.

**Divestment.** Digital-first strategy, cloud systems, three-year funded roadmap. 1.8x PCA. Announced 21 June 2021.

## Results

- Combined ratio from 101.5% to 89.2% average pre-divestment
- Motor profitable within 15 months (never previously profitable)
- NPS from +15 to +45 within two years
- Staff engagement from 50% to 70% within two years
- Digital sales from 20% to 70%, headcount held flat
- Customer remediations refunded proactively
- CANSTAR Outstanding Value five consecutive years
- Royal Commission: zero enforceable undertakings, zero fines, zero capital consequences
- ASIC Pricing Promise Review signed on time, no regulatory action
- Reinsurance $1.1bn to $1.9bn
- Divestment at 1.8x PCA with funded roadmap
