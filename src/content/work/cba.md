## Context

Commonwealth Bank's general insurance business served one million customers with more than $1 billion in gross written premium. The business operated through a partnership model across home, motor and landlord insurance, distributed through CBA's branch, digital and contact centre channels. I led a team of 700 people across underwriting, pricing, claims, distribution, product and operations.

This period included the Royal Commission into Misconduct in the Banking, Superannuation and Financial Services Industry, the COVID-19 pandemic, record catastrophe seasons, and the eventual divestment of the insurance operation to Hollard.

## What I did

**Navigating the Royal Commission.** Led the insurance business through one of the most disruptive regulatory events in Australian financial services history. Ensured the business met its obligations while maintaining commercial performance and staff morale. Staff feedback consistently noted clarity of purpose, connection to customers and shared accountability for outcomes.

**Pricing and portfolio management.** Developed a new behavioural rating factor that reduced premiums by 10% for customers while simultaneously improving the loss ratio by 1%. CANSTAR recognised the product for outstanding value five consecutive years. Reduced peril exposure through improved data sources, cost and capital allocation models and granular monitoring.

**Digital transformation.** Grew digital sales by more than 20% annually for five consecutive years, reaching 70% of total volume. Stopped the motor portfolio from shrinking by combining digital channel optimisation, targeted marketing and pricing adjustments.

**Claims and operations.** Integrated weather forecasting, predictive workforce planning, supplier allocation and digital claim services. This finalised event claims faster and at lower cost than industry averages.

**Risk and conduct.** Uplifted risk management, strengthened operational governance and ran a disciplined remedial action plan. Improved customer outcomes and reduced regulatory risk.

**Divestment and transition.** Managed the commercial and operational aspects of the transition to Hollard, ensuring continuity for one million customers.

## Results

- $1 billion premium managed across home, motor and landlord
- 700-person team led through Royal Commission, pandemic and divestment
- CANSTAR outstanding value five consecutive years
- Rating factor reduced premiums 10% while improving loss ratio 1%
- NPS improved from +15 to +45
- Digital sales grew by more than 20% annually for five years
- Event claims finalised faster and at lower cost than industry averages
- Motor portfolio decline reversed
